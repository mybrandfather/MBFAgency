About the House

Craft first.
Technology in service of it.

MyBrandFather is a creative technology agency built on a simple conviction: a brand is not a logo, a website, or a campaign. It is the accumulated weight of every decision a business makes in public. Our work is to make that weight felt.

The story

The practice behind MBF began in 2012, when our founding brand designer, Imran S. Khan, started what would become fourteen years of professional work in logo design, illustration, and brand identity. That path led through senior brand designer and illustrator roles with US-based companies in Florida, hundreds of identities, and a portfolio spanning industries from hospitality to technology.

MyBrandFather is that experience, formalized. One house where brand, digital experience, and applied AI are designed together — because a beautiful identity with a slow website is a broken promise, and a fast website with a forgettable identity is a missed one.

Three studios, one standard

01 — Brand Studio

Identity with weight

Logo systems, visual identity, packaging, and creative direction. Marks built to outlive trends, not chase them.

02 — Digital Studio

Experiences that move

Websites, ecommerce, and applications — cinematic in feel, engineered underneath, and measured by what they convert.

03 — AI Studio

A working AI workforce

Voice agents, assistants, and automation that answer, book, and follow up while you sleep. Intelligence applied to revenue, not novelty.

The Lab

We build our own products in public. LogoViking, RevenueViking, and ZaynClock are not side projects — they are proof of practice. Every tool we ship teaches us something we bring back to client work: how people actually use design tools, how AI agents behave with real callers, how a product earns trust in its first ten seconds.

What we believe

  • Restraint is a luxury signal. The premium feeling comes from what a brand declines to do.
  • Craft compounds. A brand system designed with care gets cheaper to use every year. One designed carelessly gets more expensive.
  • AI is a workforce, not a gimmick. If an automation does not answer a call, book a meeting, or recover revenue, it does not ship.
  • Honesty over theatre. We tell clients what will not work before they pay to find out.
