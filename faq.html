Questions, Answered

Frequently asked
questions.

Straight answers to the questions we hear most. If yours is not here, write to us — a real person reads every message.

What does MyBrandFather actually do?

We are a creative technology agency with three studios under one roof. The Brand Studio designs identities: logo systems, visual identity, packaging, and creative direction. The Digital Studio builds websites, ecommerce, and applications. The AI Studio deploys working AI systems: voice agents that answer your phone, assistants that handle routine work, and automation that connects the two. Most clients engage one studio first and expand once the results arrive.

How does an engagement begin?

With a conversation, not a pitch. You tell us where the business is and where it should be. We ask questions, and if we believe we can move you there, we prepare a written proposal with a defined scope, timeline, and investment. If we do not believe we are the right fit, we say so and, where we can, point you to someone who is.

What does it cost to work with you?

Every engagement is scoped individually, so we do not publish price lists. Investment depends on what is being built: a focused identity refresh is a different undertaking than a full brand, website, and AI system designed together. What we can promise is that the number is stated plainly in the proposal, it does not change mid-project unless the scope does, and you will never encounter a surprise line item.

How long does a project take?

A brand identity typically runs four to eight weeks. A custom website runs six to twelve weeks depending on scope and content readiness. AI voice agents and automation usually deploy in two to four weeks, including training and testing on your real call scenarios. Combined engagements are sequenced so strategy decisions are made once and inform everything downstream.

Who will I actually work with?

The people who do the work. MBF is founder-led: our founding brand designer brings fourteen years of professional identity and illustration experience, including senior design roles with US-based companies. You will not be handed to a junior team after the contract is signed, because there is no separate sales team to hand you off from.

Your portfolio shows work from previous roles. Why?

Because honesty matters more than optics. MBF as a house is newer than the craft inside it. The portfolio shows fourteen years of real work our team created across senior positions and freelance engagements before MBF existed. We would rather show you a decade of verifiable craft than a padded agency reel.

Do you work with clients outside the United States?

Yes. We work remotely with clients wherever they are. Engagements run on scheduled video calls, a shared project space, and written summaries after every milestone, so time zones are a scheduling detail rather than an obstacle.

What is the AI Studio, in plain terms?

It builds employees made of software. An AI voice agent answers your phone around the clock, asks the questions a good dispatcher would ask, books appointments into your real calendar, and sends your team a summary. Automation connects the tools you already use so information moves without anyone copying and pasting. If a system does not answer a call, book a meeting, or recover revenue, we do not recommend building it.

Will an AI voice agent sound robotic to my customers?

Modern voice agents hold natural conversations, and most callers simply experience a helpful person who answered on the first ring. That said, we configure every agent to be honest if asked, to follow hard rules about what it may promise, and to hand the call to a human the moment a situation calls for one. The goal is not to trick your customers. It is to stop losing them to voicemail.

Who owns the work when the project ends?

You do. Ownership of the final approved deliverables transfers to you on receipt of final payment: logo files, source design files, website code, and the accounts your AI systems run on. We retain the right to show completed work in our portfolio unless we agree to confidentiality in writing.

What happens after launch?

Nothing we build is abandoned at the finish line. Websites are handed over with documentation, and we offer ongoing care for updates, content, and performance if you want it. AI systems include a tuning period after deployment, because the first weeks of real calls always teach us something the test scripts did not.

Can you work with our existing brand rather than replacing it?

Yes, and we often should. If your identity has equity, the responsible move is to strengthen it: tighten the system, extend it to the places it was never designed for, and build the website and AI layer around it. A full rebrand is recommended only when the current identity is actively costing you business.

How many revisions are included?

Each proposal states the included revision rounds for its deliverables, and our process makes heavy revision rare: we align on strategy and direction before design begins, so you are approving refinements rather than reacting to surprises. When genuinely new requirements appear mid-project, we scope them honestly as additions instead of quietly absorbing them into a worse result.

What are LogoViking, RevenueViking, and ZaynClock?

Products we designed, built, and operate ourselves. LogoViking is a self-serve design toolkit. RevenueViking deploys AI voice agents for home services businesses. ZaynClock is a consumer product. They matter to clients for one reason: we practice what we sell, with our own money on the line.
